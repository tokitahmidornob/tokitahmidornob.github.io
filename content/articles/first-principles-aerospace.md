### The Fundamental Breakdown

In the pursuit of advanced aerospace systems, we often become entirely consumed by the mechanical hardware—thrust, drag, computational fluid dynamics, and material tensile strength. However, any system designed to carry or support human life must ultimately answer to the constraints of biology. 

Through my independent studies into anatomy, physiology, and pathology, I have realized that the human body is the most complex mechanical system we will ever encounter.

### The H.O.L.M.E.S. Approach

When we approach a problem from *first principles*, we boil it down to the fundamental truths that cannot be deduced any further. 
1. **The Machine:** What are the absolute thermodynamic and structural limits of our materials?
2. **The Operator:** What are the absolute biological and physiological limits of the human pilot?

By dismantling the silos between software (C/C++ architecture), hardware, and the human machine, we open up entirely new avenues for innovation. This is the core philosophy driving my current research and the foundation of the H.O.L.M.E.S. initiative.
