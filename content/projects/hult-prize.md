## Executive Summary
This initiative outlines our competitive strategy for the Hult Prize, focusing on engineering a scalable, sustainable business model that addresses a critical global health challenge. Our approach treats social entrepreneurship not just as a business venture, but as a systems engineering problem requiring precise, cross-disciplinary integration.

## The Target Challenge
* **The Core Problem:** Global medical imports for bone scaffolds and surgical meshes are prohibitively expensive for developing nations like Bangladesh. Simultaneously, our national "Golden Fiber" (Jute) is severely undervalued, relegated primarily to simple packaging materials.
* **The Impact Vector:** By failing to utilize indigenous, highly versatile materials, we face a dual crisis of economic stagnation in the agricultural sector and inaccessible advanced healthcare for the general population.

## The Cross-Disciplinary Solution: Project "Jute-Graft"
Our enterprise is built on a dual-pillar leadership structure, combining physical hardware with digital infrastructure to manufacture "Medical-Grade Cellulose Composites."

### 1. Mechanical & Systems Architecture (Toki Tahmid)
* **Hardware Prototyping:** Designing a high-precision Mechanical Decorticator and Thermal-Press to extract and stabilize jute fibers.
* **Biomedical Integration:** Engineering the extracted cellulose into biodegradable, biocompatible structural scaffolds for advanced tissue engineering and bone grafting.

### 2. Digital Infrastructure & Integration (CSE Co-Lead)
* **Biomechanical Simulation Engine:** Building a Finite Element Analysis (FEA) tool utilizing C++ and Linear Algebra.
* **Predictive Degradation:** The engine will accurately predict how the mechanical jute-scaffold will degrade and integrate within the human body over time.
* **Clinical Deployment App:** Developing a digital interface allowing doctors to input patient parameters and "custom-order" biologically optimized scaffolds.

## Competitive Advantage & Global Impact
This initiative directly targets **SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being)** and **SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure)**. "Jute-Graft" transforms a 19th-century crop into a 22nd-century medical miracle. By bridging the gap between mechanical engineering and computer science, we prove that we are not just engineers building an app—we are scientists engineering a biomedical revolution.
