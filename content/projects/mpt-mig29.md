## Overview: The Energy Crisis Solution
With the anticipated depletion of natural fuel resources over the next fifty years, transitioning to a technology that can power vehicles and machinery without the dependency on finite resources or cumbersome battery storage becomes imperative. 

Under the designation **Team MIG 29**, this project successfully engineered a prototype for Wireless Electricity Transmission by Microwave (MPT). This technology offers a feasible approach for addressing energy needs in both urban and remote areas, completely bypassing physical charging infrastructure.

## Technical Architecture
The system relies on a three-stage transmission framework:

* **The Transmitter (Magnetron):** A specialized vacuum tube converts standard electrical energy into high-frequency microwave radiation. Operating between 2.45 GHz and 5.8 GHz, the system utilizes an integrated transformer to amplify a 220V input to an output exceeding 2200V, dramatically enhancing transmission efficiency.
* **The Medium:** The energy is beamed through the air via line-of-sight transmission. Directional techniques ensure the beam is guided precisely, reducing energy dispersion and scatter.
* **The Receiver (Rectenna Array):** A grid of receiving antennas captures the microwave energy from the air and channels it into a rectifier. The rectifier then converts the high-frequency alternating current (AC) back into a stable direct current (DC) to power the target device.

## Mathematical Modeling & Physics
To calculate the exact power received over distance, the system architecture utilizes the **Friis Transmission Equation**:

$$P_{r}=P_{t} \cdot \left(\frac{\lambda}{4\pi d}\right)^{2} \cdot G_{t} \cdot G_{r}$$

Where $P_{r}$ is the power at the receiver, $P_{t}$ is the transmitted power, $\lambda$ represents the microwave wavelength, and $d$ is the distance.

Furthermore, the safe and efficient intensity of the beam is governed by the Power Density ($S$) calculation:

$$S=\frac{P_{t} \cdot G_{t}}{4\pi d^{2}}$$
