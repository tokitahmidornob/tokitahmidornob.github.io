## Overview: The Energy Crisis Solution
[cite_start]With the anticipated depletion of natural fuel resources over the next fifty years, transitioning to a technology that can power vehicles and machinery without the dependency on finite resources or cumbersome battery storage becomes imperative[cite: 7]. 

Under the designation **Team MIG 29**, this project successfully engineered a prototype for Wireless Electricity Transmission by Microwave (MPT). [cite_start]This technology offers a feasible approach for addressing energy needs in both urban and remote areas, completely bypassing physical charging infrastructure[cite: 8, 15].

## Technical Architecture
The system relies on a three-stage transmission framework:

* [cite_start]**The Transmitter (Magnetron):** A specialized vacuum tube converts standard electrical energy into high-frequency microwave radiation[cite: 30, 31]. [cite_start]Operating between 2.45 GHz and 5.8 GHz, the system utilizes an integrated transformer to amplify a 220V input to an output exceeding 2200V, dramatically enhancing transmission efficiency[cite: 32, 33].
* **The Medium:** The energy is beamed through the air via line-of-sight transmission. [cite_start]Directional techniques ensure the beam is guided precisely, reducing energy dispersion and scatter[cite: 68, 69].
* [cite_start]**The Receiver (Rectenna Array):** A grid of receiving antennas captures the microwave energy from the air and channels it into a rectifier[cite: 55, 56]. [cite_start]The rectifier then converts the high-frequency alternating current (AC) back into a stable direct current (DC) to power the target device[cite: 57].

## Mathematical Modeling & Physics
[cite_start]To calculate the exact power received over distance, the system architecture utilizes the **Friis Transmission Equation**[cite: 81, 83]:

$P_{r}=P_{t}.(\frac{\lambda}{4\pi d})^{2}.G_{t}.G_{r}$

[cite_start]Where $P_{r}$ is the power at the receiver, $P_{t}$ is the transmitted power, $\lambda$ represents the microwave wavelength, and $d$ is the distance[cite: 85, 86, 87, 88].

[cite_start]Furthermore, the safe and efficient intensity of the beam is governed by the Power Density ($S$) calculation[cite: 91]:

$S=\frac{P_{t}.G_{t}}{4\pi d^{2}}$
